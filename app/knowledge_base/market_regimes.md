# Market Regimes

Market regimes describe the broad condition of the market at a given time.

Common regimes:
- Bull market: prices and momentum are generally strong.
- Bear market: prices trend lower and drawdowns deepen.
- Sideways market: returns are muted and range-bound.
- High volatility: market swings widen and portfolio risk rises.

In this product, the market regime does not replace the user's long-term plan. Instead, it slightly adjusts portfolio exposure to make the recommendation more defensive or more growth-oriented while staying within the chosen risk band.
