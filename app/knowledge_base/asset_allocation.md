# Asset Allocation Basics

Asset allocation is the process of spreading a portfolio across different asset classes such as stocks, bonds, REITs, and alternatives.

Why it matters:
- Stocks generally support long-term growth but can be more volatile.
- Bonds tend to reduce drawdowns and smooth portfolio behavior.
- REITs can add diversification through real estate exposure.
- Alternatives may help in specific market environments but should not dominate a long-term plan.

A portfolio should be aligned with the investor's horizon, savings behavior, and ability to tolerate volatility.
